Protective Areas Issues

Protected areas, designated to conserve biodiversity and natural heritage, face a myriad of issues that threaten their integrity and effectiveness. Despite their critical role in preserving ecosystems, species, and genetic diversity, these areas are increasingly challenged by external pressures such as illegal logging, poaching, climate change, and encroachment through agriculture and urban development. Furthermore, internal challenges such as insufficient funding, inadequate management, and conflicts with local communities over land use rights and resource access exacerbate the situation. As the global community strives towards conservation goals, understanding and addressing these issues is paramount for the sustainability and resilience of protected areas worldwide.

    Coral reefs harbour the highest biodiversity of any ecosystem globally and directly support over 500 million people worldwide, mostly in poor countries.
